2019 has witnessed an increase in the adverse effects of the humanitarian crises on people's health. Regarding the crisis in the Lake Chad basin, cholera, measles and polio epidemics were recorded with a very high risk of resurgence in 2020. Similarly, attacks by Boko Haram continue to generate injured and dead, and also target the livelihoods of populations, thereby reducing their ability to meet their health expenses, in an environment with the highest morbidity rates linked to diseases and climatic hazards in the country. Furthermore, the rate of births attended by qualified personnel remains very low in the Far North region (less than 35%) thus increasing the rate of maternal and neonatal deaths.
