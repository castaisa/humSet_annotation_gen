Insecurity in Al Hudaydah prompted at least seven health facilities to close in recent weeks, while other health activities have been hampered by access constraints, restrictions on mobile health team operations, a lack of community health volunteers in the governorate, and insufficient capacity to respond to mass casualties and trauma cases, according to WHO. Moreover, the UN agency reports two of Al Hudaydah city’s primary public hospitals are at increased risk of damage if fighting reaches the city. To increase hospital capacity, WHO delivered 40 inpatient beds, nine intensive care unit beds, and 5.6 million liters of water to three public hospitals in the city from June 24–30.
