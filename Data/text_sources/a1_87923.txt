For the majority of IDPs, 60 per cent, that live in private rented accommodation, increasing rental costs and lack of access to cash, has made it more difficult for families to make rent payments, putting them at greater risk of eviction by authorities or landlords, particularly those without official rental agreements65. Results from the two most recent displacement tracking rounds indicate an uptick in IDPs staying with host families (2 per cent increase), rather than in rented accommodation, pointing both to an erosion of coping capacity and the increasingly protracted nature of displacement that is impacting on families’ ability to continue paying for rented accommodation.
