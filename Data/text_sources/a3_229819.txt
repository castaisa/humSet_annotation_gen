Attendance rates continue to be lower than enrolment rates, with 29 per cent of girls and 35 per cent of boys reportedly attending school for more than 3 days per week in the three months prior to data collection . Low attendance is attributed to a variety of factors such as poor quality of education, including lack of appropriate learning facilities, no or minimal learning materials, untrained teachers and overcrowded classrooms. The latest EMIS report for the southern and central regions indicates that only 35 per cent of the assessed primary school teachers are qualified. The EMIS report also shows that at primary level the pupil textbook ratio in some regions is as poor as 1:74 .
