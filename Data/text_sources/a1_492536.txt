[1 to 21 September 2021] As of 21 September, approximately 28,000 of the 36,000 people displaced from Dar’a Al-Balad and other areas have returned to their places of origin. This includes the estimated 1,400 IDPs in shelters in Dar’a AlMahatta and 2,000 residents of the camps in Dar’a Al-Balad. Movement restrictions on civilians have largely eased within Dar’a Al-Balad, the Nassib crossing to Jordan has reopened for civilian and commercial movement. The Sijneh and Saraya crossings were reopened on 6 September resulting in steady returns. Civilian movement is reportedly back to normal although explosive hazard contamination is hampering returns.
