Yemen has been the poorest country of the Middle East and North Africa region before the current crisis and ranked 168 out of 188 countries (2016 UNDP Human Development Index). During 2017, Yemen continued to be besieged by multiple armed conflicts and other situations of violence, with the insecurity escalating in many parts of the country, between the al Houthis and the coalition that supports the government of Yemen. According to the global INFORM index, Yemen is ranked as number five out of 191 countries. Yemen has a hazard and exposure index of 8.1, a vulnerability index of 6.9 and a lack of coping capacity index of 7.9. After nearly two and a half years since the escalation of the conflict, the resilience and the coping capacity of people of Yemen have been remarkable, however exhausted beyond comprehension. Prior to the crisis, almost half of the Yemeni population already lived below the poverty line, with a high rate of unemployment and the basic social services on the verge to collapse. Violations of international humanitarian law and human rights law are reportedly widespread, in addition to a long history of food insecurity, malnutrition, and lack of access to water, sanitation and poor healthcare.
