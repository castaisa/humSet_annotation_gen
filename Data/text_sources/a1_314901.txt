In January 2021, UNICEF through the support of partners provided curative consultations nearly 135,000 people, including over 70,000 children under the age of 5 years. The figure includes 72,850 females and 63,316 males. Patients were reached through static health facilities, routine outreach activities and Boma Health Initiative (BHI) modalities. Most children and adults received consultations for malaria (37%) followed by pneumonia (17%) and diarrhoea (14%). In addition, UNICEF and its partners distributed 98,634 long lasting insecticide treated nets (LLITNs) to 49,317 families. UNICEF also continued with the distribution of essential drugs and medical equipment to the 191 World Bank supported health facilities in the 25 counties in Upper Nile and Jonglei States.
