These developmental and behavioural factors are especially important to consider in an environment with many unprotected, open water bodies like the camps. A further key influence is suggested to be a lack of adequate supervision. Inadequate supervision was an underlying factor in each of the fatalities to children under 5. In these cases, caregivers were undertaking essential activities that caused distractions from direct child supervision, which led to an increased risk of the child drowning (Borse et al, 2011).
