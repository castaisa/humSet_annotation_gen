In the directorate of Mudaraba, one of Lahj's directorates, 8,000 African refugees live; most of them come from Somalia after crossing the Gulf of Aden to reach Yemen. They are among thousands of refugees, some of whom were horribly killed during the trip, either drowning or suffocating due to overcrowding in the boats. There is a small number of refugees in Sana'a, but they are mainly concentrated in Kharaz camp, which was established as a temporary solution for refugees from the Horn of Africa, until the end of the conflicts in their countries, especially Somalia. The camp is a number of buildings provided by the UN High Commissioner for Refugees; it includes schools and clinics and food rations are allocated to it.
