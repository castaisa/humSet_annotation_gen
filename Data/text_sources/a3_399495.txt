[12th Aug 2021, Nigeria]  Internally displaced people—Direct interventions are undertaken with local and national authorities to ensure that internally displaced people (IDPs) in camps or out of camps live in safety. In the North-East, UNHCR established a community-based structure composed of 17 protection coordinators, some 100 protection monitors, nearly 50 human rights monitors and 250 protection action group members to deliver protection services such as protection monitoring, vulnerability screening, capacity building and access to justice, while ensuring age, gender and diversity mainstreaming. Since the beginning of 2021, almost 148,000 IDPs benefitted from awareness campaigns on GBV and COVID-19.
