For households engaged in family businesses, the fall in revenue was pronounced. Four in ten respondents in the household survey (41%) reported that a household member operated a family business (in agriculture or other activities). Of those, more than half (58%) indicated that since the beginning of the outbreak, their income from the family business was less than usual (42%) or that there was no income at all (16% – Table 8). This revenue loss was attributed to several different factors, but the most significant by far were a lack of customers (52%) and the usual place of business being closed due to public health restrictions imposed by the authorities (49%).
