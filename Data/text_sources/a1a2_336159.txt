[18 May, 2021, Cox's Bazar]Refugee/Host: Besides the health risks posed by the virus, lockdowns to reduce its spread have hit the livelihoods of locals, and the ability of refugees to supplement the assistance they receive from aid organizations. The result has been increasing levels of food insecurity in both populations. A recent assessment by the World Food Programme found that by the end of 2020, 86 per cent or Rohingya refugees were highly vulnerable to poverty and hunger, up from 70 per cent in 2019.Vulnerability also rose in the local community, with 51 per cent considered moderately or highly vulnerable compared to 41 per cent the previous year.
