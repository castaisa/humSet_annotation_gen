When looking at population groups in the state, IDP and returnee households were found to be in greater need of food and agriculture assistance compared to non-displaced households – respectively 82% and 81% vs. 66%. Based on previous observations, the most likely reason for this higher proportion could include the displacement status of those households, who might have limited access to agriculture opportunities and fewer resources to access food items. One of the main drivers of high needs for IDPs was a higher proportion of households having poor or borderline FCS (83%) compared to non-displaced and returnee households (58% and 57% respectively).
