[July-August 2020, Bangladesh]  Rohingya Refugees:     These findings represent a continuation of the 2019 findings  in the sense that while almost all individuals reported as having required treatment did seek treatment, when needed, households did frequently adopt coping mechanisms in order to deal with health concerns, including paying for health care, going into debt and seeking lower quality treatment. However, compared to 2019, the proportion of households that paid for health care decreased from 57% to 41%, and the proportion of households that went into debt to cover health expenses decreased from 66% to 35%, while the proportion of households that sought lower quality/cheaper treatment increased from 12% to 27%.
