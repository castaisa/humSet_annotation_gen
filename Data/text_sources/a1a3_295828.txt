An escalation of sub-national violence and internal displacement appears to have created food access gaps in Tonj South County. Despite the seasonal harvest, the proportion of assessed settlements reporting adequate access to food remained comparable in Tonj South during this period (57% in July versus 53% in September). This minimal improvement in access to food may be explained by conflict and displacement, which according to an inter-agency assessment, prevented 36% of the population in Manyang Ngok, Wanalel and Tonj payams of Tonj South County from engaging in cultivation in the last planting season. With such a high proportion of population that did not cultivate, access to food will likely remain a concern in the county in the next lean season.
