Since the Venezuelan government started creating obstacles for citizens wanting to leave the country, obtaining a passport can entail not only an extremely lengthy procedure, but also a high financial cost – from a few hundred to several thousand US dollars – in bribes to public officials.59 This series of restrictions to the movement of Venezuelan refugees and migrants risks having one, or both, of the following consequences: an increase in irregular migration, if people on the move decide to head to these countries regardless of their entry requirements; and/or Colombia – which cannot effectively close its border with Venezuela – will continue to be left carrying the biggest burden of this migration crisis.
