Food Imports: Prior to the blockade (July 2016 to October 2017), nearly all (96%) of Yemen’s monthly food import requirements were being met on average. Since the blockade, monthly food imports had met 70% of national requirements. In March, food imports were 57% of the monthly national requirement being met each month on average. Since November, monthly fuel imports had met 20% of national requirements on average. In March, fuel imports were one-third (33%) of the monthly national requirement. Food prices: Food prices are 47% higher than pre-crisis. Food and fuel prices available in previous issue dated 15 March 2018.
