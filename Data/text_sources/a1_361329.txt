[21 June 2021, Cox's Bazar]Refugee: Currently, there are approximately 877,000 displaced Rohingya residing in camps in the Ukhiya and Teknaf Upazilas of Cox’s Bazar District (UNHCR, 2021). More than half of the refugees in the camp are children (Vince, 2020). There are thirty-four camps formally designated by the government of Bangladesh, including the two officially recognised camps, Kutupalong and Nayapara, as well as several camps supported by agencies such as the International Organis ation for Migration (IOM) and the UN Refugee Agency (UNHCR) (UNHCR, 2020; Banerjee, 2019; Milton et al, 2017). Along with existing challenges in addressing access to health services, food shortages and education needs (Pocock et al, 2017), the risks posed by waterrelated hazards for those living in the camps are high. Cox’s Bazar is a district that is prone to floods, storm surges and the seasonal monsoon impact, whilst the refugee camps and their surrounding areas are highly susceptible to flash flooding and rainfall triggered landslides (Banerjee, 2019; Ahmed, 2015).
