[21st Dec2020,Nigeria]Furthermore, flood-related damage to transportation infrastructure across northern Nigeria—in addition to ongoing conflict in flood-affected areas of the northeast—has further restricted relief actors’ movements in the region and hampered response efforts in areas requiring urgent assistance, the UN reports. Flooding also caused significant damage to cropland throughout northwestern Nigeria, limiting livelihoods for agro-pastoral households recovering from the June-to-August lean season. Floodwaters completely submerged croplands in Kebbi—where most of Nigeria’s rice is grown—and caused damage in 18 of 27 LGAs in agriculturally-dependent Jigawa, according to IFRC.
