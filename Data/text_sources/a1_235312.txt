Several counties have elevated levels of poor and borderline food consumption including Juba (91 percent ), Akobo (96 percent ), Canal/Pigi (100 percent ), Nyirol (94 percent ), Pibor (97 percent ), Rumbek North (96 percent ) Bailet (94 percent ), Maban (99 percent ) and Nagero (95 percent ) where 9 in 10 households consumed very poor diets. Similarly, at least 8 in 10 households in the counties of Kajo-keji, Lainya, Terekeka, Magwi, Ayod, Awerial, Rumbek Centre, Wulu, Yirol East, Leer, Malakal, Ulang, Raja, Wau and Ibba had nutritionally inadequate diets consisting mostly of cereals with very little else during the week preceding the assessment.(December 2019)
