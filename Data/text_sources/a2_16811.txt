Despite the Humanitarian Food Assistance provided in Al Bayda, Al Dhale’e, Al Hudaydah, Al Mahwit, Amran, Hajjah, Saada’a and Taiz, there exist presence of pockets of households experiencing extreme food gaps and exhaustion of coping strategies. Food security outcomes portray an extremely dire situation where more than half of the population are in the most affected districts are classified in IPC Phase 4 (Emergency) and 45 districts with nearly 240,000 population are classified in IPC Phase 5 (Catastrophe) based on extremely high proportions of population with poor food score and household hunger score in the absence of Humanitarian food assistance.
