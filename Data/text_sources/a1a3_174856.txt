The government began delivery of the IS transfer in the second week of April, with the goal of reaching all recipients with bank accounts before the end of the month.169 By April 22nd, just over one- third, 1 163 million households, had been reached. Meanwhile, in mid-April, protests broke out across cities in Colombia in response to lack of food, and opposition politicians called on the government to do more.170 At the same time, reports of incorrect recipients and potential fraud surfaced, with an audit revealing 35,000 irregularities in registration for benefits.171 In response, the government noted that 99.4% of the registrations were correct and that those that were not had been removed.172
