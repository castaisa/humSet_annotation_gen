According to OCHA, between January and May 2018, a monthly average of 7.16 million people were provided with emergency food assistance through in-kind distributions, cash transfers and vouchers. Moreover, about 871,000 beneficiaries got livelihoods assistance including agricultural, livestock and fisheries inputs. Assistance resumed in Aden, after being suspended since January 2018, and WFP provided food assistance to about 82,000 individuals in June. Since May, the number of beneficiaries reached has increased. WFP reported that approximately 7.6 million people in June were provided with general food assistance through in-kind food and commodity-vouchers. This represents the largest number of beneficiaries humanitarian partners have been able to reach to date.
