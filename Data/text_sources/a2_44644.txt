Gwoza LGA had the greatest proportion of households categorized with “high” or “very high” needs (39%), compared to other East Borno LGAs, due in large part to unmet shelter/NFI (84%), livelihoods (71%), food security (63%), protection (38%), WASH (16%), and nutrition (15%) needs. Much of these needs are due to recent influxes of IDPs and returnees to Gwoza and Pulka towns since the beginning of 2018, making it difficult for humanitarian actors to keep up with meeting basic needs . More than half of households reported insecurity as a barrier to accessing agricultural land (57%), highlighting how insecurity in the area is contributing to poor livelihoods.
