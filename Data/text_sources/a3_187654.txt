Children are being kept out of school for a range of reasons including security issues and cultural beliefs. Attacks on schools have undermined the right of children to a safe and secure environment, compromising their physical and psychological well-being. 2019 has also seen a high number of schools attacked and closed as a result of election-related violence, general insecurity, cross-fire, intimidation and threats disrupting and diminishing children’s access to essential education. As of October 2019, 722 schools had been forcibly closed and remained closed due to insecurity, affecting the education of around 328,094 children (113,597 girls and 214,497 boys).
