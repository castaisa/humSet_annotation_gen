The security situation in North-East Nigeria remains volatile due to sporadic attacks by Non-State Armed Groups (NSAGs) on civilian and military infrastructure in Borno and Adamawa states. On 7 May 2019, NSAG elements attacked two villages (Molai and Maiboriti) about 4 kilometers from Maiduguri - the state capital. During the attack, at least 10 people including military personnel were reportedly killed, several others wounded and houses set ablaze by the attackers. The incident forced residents of the areas and nearby communities to flee to the main city center and into IDP camps. Intermittent gunshots and explosions continued during the night of the incident.
