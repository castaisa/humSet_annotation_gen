[1st Aug-Sep 2020,Borno state]Linked to implied exhaustion of coping strategies, a few KIs directly mentioned coping strategies that they used to apply but were no longer able to. These coping strategies included selling livestock (1), eating stored food (1) or getting support from neighbours or relatives (6). In addition to the KIs describing people in their settlements no longer being able to rely on neighbours or relatives for support, 10 KIs mentioned not being able to get support from neighbours or relatives in general without describing if this had ever been an option. Of those describing getting support from neighbours or relatives in the past some KIs mentioned a decrease in the food available as the reason for this (see quote below).
