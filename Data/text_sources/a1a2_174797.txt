As Figure 13 indicates, pre-pandemic, Colombia had 30% of the population in poverty, compared to Ecuador’s 24%, and in extreme poverty, 11% compared to Ecuador’s 7%. Colombia has 39% of its workers covered in social security, while Ecuador has 42%. The share of households with access to electricity is over 98% in both countries, while access to water in Ecuador is 86%, and in Colombia 90%. Exactly half of households in Colombia have access to internet, while in Ecuador the share is 37%. A higher share of the urban population in Ecuador lives in slums at 36%, compared to Colombia, at just 13%.
