[8 August 2021, Bangladesh] One in ten households reported problems in accessing general healthcare services during the pandemic: The respondents were asked whether they faced any problem during the lockdown in accessing general healthcare services. One in ten households (9.9%) stated that they faced problems during getting treatment from hospitals. The problems include lack of sufficient doctors (70.2%), lack of sufficient nurse (39.6%), lack of medical equipment (38.8%), lack of medicine (38.0%), lack of beds (26.1%) and higher fees of doctors (20.4%). They also faced the unwillingness of doctors to provide treatment (23.3%), the unwillingness of hospitals to admit patients (20.0%) and bribery (13.9%). People from rural areas complained more about the lack of doctors (75.0% vs. 63.8%) and nurses (42.9% vs. 35.2%), adequate medical equipment (40.0% vs. 37.1%), beds (29.3% vs. 21.9%) and increase of doctor’s fees (23.6% vs.16.2%) compared to people from urban areas
