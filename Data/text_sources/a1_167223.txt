The insecure financial situation of migrant households is underscored by the observation that “many refugee children do not come to school regularly” and “some parents needed their child to contribute to the family’s income instead”.37 Issues of exploitation and intimidation at work have also been reported.38 Reporting of these issues to the authorities is low, at less than 40 cases in the last four years, due to the lack of knowledge of reporting procedures and “fear of incarceration or deportation due to migrants’ irregular immigration status”.39 The overall picture of the migrant employment situation is one of high unemployment, low-paid and insecure jobs, and discrimination.
