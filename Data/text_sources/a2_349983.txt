[April 2021, NWS] As a result of water shortage, households resorted to spending money usually spent on other things or buying water on credit in 43% and 27% of the communities, respectively. Moreover, to cope with the lack of sufficient water, households in around 40% of the communities reportedly bathed less frequently and did laundry less frequently. Furthermore, reducing drinking water consumption was reported by KIs as a coping strategy in 7% of the assessed communities. In fact, drinking water was indicated as a water, sanitation and hygiene (WASH) priority need in 40% of the assessed communities in Northern Aleppo and in 28% of communities in Greater Idleb.
