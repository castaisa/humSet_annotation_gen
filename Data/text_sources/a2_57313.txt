UNICEF funded and participated in a government led multi-agency needs assessment mission to the border gateway community of Kaikan in Region 7, from 25 to 27 June involving interactions with migrants at six sites. Limited data had been available for the national coordination mechanism prior to this mission, other than anecdotal references to a steady transient flow across the border. All 70+ migrants engaged had travelled for a minimum of three days from urban areas without accompanying family, and were between the ages of 20 to 40, with most having children left in Venezuela often located just across the border that relied on the income they were generating
