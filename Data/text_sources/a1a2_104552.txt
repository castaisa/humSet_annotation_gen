As COVID-19 has spread in Latin America and the Caribbean (LAC), Ministries of Education have progressively closed schools at the preschool, primary and secondary levels. As of 21 April, schools had been closed in 23 countries and 12 independent states in the region. As a result, it is estimated that more than 159 million children and adolescents5 have been affected in the region, representing more than 95% of the students enrolled throughout the region. The suspension of education services has impacted all education systems in the world, and the LAC region has not been spared; this has meant the emergence of multiple education in emergencies responses by governments. These initiatives seek to ensure the quality of education and to give continuity to their school calendars, using various resources and methodologies.
