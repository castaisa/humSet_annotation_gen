To mitigate child protection risks and support children’s access to inter-sectoral programming, 10 CFS were established by local partners with UNICEF support and another 8 by other organizations in highly populated communities in Trinidad between 2018 and 2019. The CFS provide children with a safe space for play, socialisation and learning, with access to education, child protection, life-saving messages, and social integration to reduce children’s risk of violence, neglect and exploitation. In these spaces, children have access to psychosocial support and are given the opportunity to restore their normal development, reduce harmful levels of stress, learn and share positive coping strategies in a supportive environment with peers, and learn valuable information about personal safety.
