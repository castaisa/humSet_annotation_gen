[November 2020, Cox's Bazar] Protection trends and situation analysis : Housing, Land and Property Task Forum (HLPTF) • Host communities continue to be the primary providers of land for refugees, particularly in the Teknaf camps. This has led to precarious tenure arrangements, with refugees paying rent from a low of zero in the older camps to a high of 500-800 BDT per month in the Teknaf camps. The majority are paying rent through CFW, in-kind (through food rations, NFI materials), and labour (working on fishing boats and agricultural farms owned by the host communities). There has been a marked increase in exploitative practises, with those who cannot pay the high rent resorting to negative coping mechanisms, including illicit activities/yaba, sexual favours/trafficking—leading to many living in constant fear of eviction and homelessness.
