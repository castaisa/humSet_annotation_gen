[17 June 2021, NWS] Idlib province, Syria’s last rebel stronghold, is home to around 3 million people, more than half of whom depend on food aid. All of that filters through the Bab al-Hawa crossing where currently around 1,000 U.N. trucks enter a month through Turkey. “Right now there is a plan for if no renewal happens and alongside our partner the World Food Programme we are stockpiling for three months until the end of September,” said Bassil al-Dirri, Idlib area manager for the Turkish Humanitarian Relief Association (IYD). “But after that there will be nothing.”
