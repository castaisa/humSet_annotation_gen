In order to mitigate the impact of the reduced coverage UNICEF has successfully negotiated with local radio stations to play pre-recorded lessons. UNICEF distributed essential learning materials and dignity kits to 495 children (220 girls and 275 boys) to support their radio learning programmes. The essential learning supplies included exercise books, pens, pencils and old curriculum textbooks. An additional 150 sitting mats were distributed to children in the Unity State cattle camps to create a conducive learning environment. The Education Cluster continued to work closely with the Ministry of General Education and Instruction (MoGEI), education partners and donors to support the resumption of education activities. The Cluster also participated in MoGEI’s workshop on Education Partners’ Mapping Tool Validation.
