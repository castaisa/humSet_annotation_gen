More than 1.84 million people are now internally displaced across the three northeast states of Adamawa, Borno and Yobe, according to the IOM DTM Round XXIX (November 2019). IDPs decreased in Adamawa and increased in Borno and Yobe, with Borno the most affected state by the conflict, having the highest number of IDPs (1,483,566 persons). Continuous displacement in the northeast region is attributed to long-term hostilities between non-state armed groups (NSAGs) and security forces (SF). This has sustained humanitarian needs in the affected communities with IDPs identifying access to water, sanitation, medical services, non-food item and food as their major needs .
