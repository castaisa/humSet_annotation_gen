[8 August 2021, Bangladesh] Around one-third of delivery carried out at home: Among the surveyed households, 106 babies were born during the pandemic situation. Half of the delivery was carried out in private clinics and around one-third of delivery (29%) was done at home. In 23% of cases, people went to government-owned health facilities such as Upazila health complexes (13%), medical college hospitals (5%) and union family planning centres (3%). In the national data, the percentage of delivery at non-institutional places is 46.4% (Unicef, 2019). The huge difference in the findings with the national data was probably the result of a small sample size.
