There are approximately 287,600 refugees in urgent need of nutrition interventions. Among these are 140,374 children below five years old; 91,702 pregnant and lactating women; 32,605 infants and young children; and 22,919 others. For refugees living in camps in East Sudan, the recent SENS survey indicates a GAM rate of 13 per cent in the Shagarab camps, and over 11 per cent for all other camps. Stunting prevalence is critical in Shagarab camps at 53 per cent, as well as for Abuda, Um Gargour and Fau 5 at 45 per cent. The SENS survey also indicate high rates of anaemia (>40 per cent) among children under five years and women aged 15-49 years.
