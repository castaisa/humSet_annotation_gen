Multi-sectoral needs in Yobe were most commonly driven by needs for services in education (77%) and health (71%), and for food security support (66%). Health and education needs were the most common to co-occur, with 57% of Yobe households demonstrating dual need for services in these sectors. Education and food security were the second most common sectors in which needs co-occurred, with 54% of all Yobe households reporting simultaneous food security and education needs. A lack of available, formal education services was the dominant component of multi-sectoral needs and, of the 77% of households with unmet needs in the education sector, 46% registered a vulnerability, such as a high age-dependency ratio or a separated child. It is notable that Yobe reported the highest percentage of households hosting separated and unaccompanied children (6%) as compared to Borno (1%) and Adamawa (2%), which could contribute to higher education needs among school-aged children and vulnerabilities among households.
