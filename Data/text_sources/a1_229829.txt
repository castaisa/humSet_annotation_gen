The JCMNA assessment reported that 27 per cent of households had some or all of their enrolled children drop out of school in the last 12 months. The main reasons are associated with the cost of school fees (36 per cent), drought (33 per cent) and work to support the household (17 per cent), while 4 per cent stated early marriage and 6 per cent stated conflict as the main reason. The main barrier to accessing education is the cost of school fees, as reported by 50 per cent of non-displaced families and 65 per cent of displaced families with children not attending school .
