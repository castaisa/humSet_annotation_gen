IDP households reported using more extensively their resources in the 30 days prior to the assessment with 75.1% reporting spending their savings as a coping mechanism to a lack of cash, in addition to having already exhausted this strategy for 12.2% of them. In comparison, the proportion of non-displaced households having used this strategy was lower (at 57.5%) with only 4.8% having already exhausted it. Similarly, a lower proportion (23.6%) of returnee households have spent savings, and 13.9% had already exhausted them. The second most reported coping strategy overall, selling gold, was reported by 54.7% of returnee, 53.8% of nondisplaced and 41.6% of IDP households having either used it in the 30 days prior to the assessment or already exhausted it. Having exhausted many of their reserves, IDP households appeared as more vulnerable as any income shock will limit their spending capacity and might force them to turn to even more negative spending- or food-related coping strategies.
