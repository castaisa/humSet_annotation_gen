In the reporting week ending January 13, 2019: o There were 85 new cases of Acute Flaccid Paralysis (AFP) reported. None was confirmed as polio. The last reported case of polio in Nigeria was in August 2016. Active case search for AFP is being intensified with the goal to eliminate polio in Nigeria. o There were 18 suspected cases of cholera reported from three LGAs in two States (Ebonyi – 9 & Kano – 9). There was no laboratory confirmed case and no death. o There were 116 suspected cases of Lassa fever reported from 16 LGAs in eight States (Bauchi –12, Edo –58, Ebonyi – 11, FCT – 2, Ondo – 27, Nasarawa – 1, Plateau – 3 & Taraba - 2). 35 were laboratory confirmed and eight deaths were recorded. o There were 22 suspected cases of Cerebrospinal Meningitis (CSM) reported from 14 LGAs in five States (Cross River – 2, Katsina – 10, Kwara – 2, Taraba - 2 & Zamfara – 6). Of these, none was laboratory confirmed and no death was recorded.o There were 503 suspected cases of measles reported from 32 States. None was laboratory confirmed and two deaths were recorded
