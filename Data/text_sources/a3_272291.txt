[1st Feb 2021, Nigeria] The total number of adults in the high risk areas is about 13.2 million (13% of the country's total adult population), of which 78% (10.2 M) are located in rural areas. About 1% of the total adult population at high risk is in ZOI (dark red; 0.9M). Half of the top ten hotspots overlap with the (unofficial) subnational reporting of confirmed cases , including Ogun, Oyo, Delta, Plateau, and Edo States. While Lagos overwhelmingly leads the number of cases, it is estimated as a low-risk area due to the younger urban population and its relatively more efficient handling of COVID-19 cases than other states .
