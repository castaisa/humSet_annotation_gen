Figure 6 illustrates the change in monthly food expenditure of a household from before the pandemic compared to after the outbreak, with refugee households spending on average nearly $2.00 more per household member. This is likely a reflection of rising food prices since the beginning of the pandemic as increased expenditures on food are seen for all other groups as well albeit to a lesser extent, aside from refugee returnees. No doubt food assistance, in the form of CBI for refugees, has played an essential role in mitigating the risk of severe food insecurity. Among both refugee and IDP households, humanitarian actors are the main provider of food assistance with around half and three-quarters, respectively, reporting receipt since the outbreak of the pandemic. Alternatively, food assistance from the government is especially significant among both returnee groups as well as host community households.
