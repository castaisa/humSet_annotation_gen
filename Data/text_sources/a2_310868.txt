Acute malnutrition remains widespread in Somalia. A Critical (IPC Phase 4) prevalence of GAM (≥ 15%) was observed in nine out of 36 population groups: Southern Inland Pastoral of Elberde district (20.9%), Mogadishu IDPs (16.7%), Huddur urban /IDPs (16.4%), IDPs in Bossasso and Garowe (15.8%), Shabelle Riverine (15.4%); and in addition, Beletweyne IDPs, Beletweyne urban and Juba Riverine have also been classified as Critical (IPC Phase 4) based on MUAC assessment results: The nutrition situation in 28 population groups was classified as Serious (IPC Phase 3), with GAM at 10–14.9%. The nutrition situation in most of the remaining population groups was Alert (IPC Phase 2), with GAM at 5-9.9%, with only Burao urban classified as Acceptable (IPC Phase 1), with GAM at <5%.
