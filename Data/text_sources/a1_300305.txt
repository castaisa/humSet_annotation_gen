Burkina Faso is a country with limited natural resources and highly vulnerable to climate change. Over the last 20 years, the state has been dependent on and affected by severe rain decrease due to the decline in water resources and soil degradation. The Notre Dame's Global Adaptation Index (ND-GAIN) measured Burkina Faso's levels of vulnerability to climate change as well as the readiness of countries to respond to this process; Burkina Faso is the 25th-most-vulnerable country of those assessed. That said, Burkina Faso was one of the first African countries to commit in signing the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change in 1993 and the Kyoto Protocol in 2005. Burkina Faso published its National Adaptation Plan (PNA) in September 2015, which focused on the most vulnerable sector, agriculture, water resources, livestock and forests/biodiversity. Since then, Burkina Faso has been able to sustainably strengthen agriculture's competitiveness in sectors other than cotton by increasing the income derived from agricultural exports and set up reforestation programs.
