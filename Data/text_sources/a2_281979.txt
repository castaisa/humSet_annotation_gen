[Survey in NW, SW, Littoral and West region, conducted on 4,638 people through one to one interview using a survey tool] The data also shows that very little cash, employment or livelihoods assistance has been provided in any region: only 7% report receiving cash assistance, 2% report receiving livelihoods assistance and 2% report receiving employment assistance. Given the fact that increase in costs of living or expenses and loss of household income have been identified as key concerns, increases in this form of assistance could strengthen coping capacities. Investing in resilience support, including through livelihoods and cash assistance, will not only reduce the risks that households face now, but also mitigate the impact of the COVD-19 in the medium- to long-term.
