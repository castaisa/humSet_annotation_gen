[1st Dec 2020, Nigeria]Households with limited access to humanitarian food assistance depend on the small quantities harvested with below-average purchasing power face Crisis (IPC Phase 3) outcomes. Households in difficult to access areas have little to no access to humanitarian food assistance. They are mainly consuming wild foods and face wider food consumption gaps, and elevated levels of malnutrition are likely facing Emergency (IPC Phase 4) outcomes. A risk of Famine (IPC Phase 5) persists in the event there is a shift in conflict that isolates households and further restricts already limited food and income sources for a prolonged period.
